Chapter 8:Information Security and Cyber Law

1. What challenges are we facing to create a digital society in the Nepalese context?

- Infrastructure: Lack of reliable internet access and advanced technology infrastructure, especially in rural areas.
- Digital Literacy: Low levels of digital literacy, limiting people's ability to use and benefit from digital services.
- Cybersecurity: Growing concerns about cybercrimes and data breaches without sufficient protections.
- Regulatory Framework: Limited or evolving legal frameworks to protect data and privacy in the digital world.
- Cost: High costs of accessing digital tools and technology for a large portion of the population.
- Power Supply: Inconsistent power supply, especially in rural and remote areas, hampering continuous digital connectivity.

2. What is computer ethics? What are the commandments of computer ethics?

Computer Ethics: A set of moral guidelines and principles that govern the use of computers and related technologies.
Commandments of Computer Ethics:

  • Thou shalt not use a computer to harm other people.
  • Thou shalt not interfere with other people's computer work.
  • Thou shalt not snoop around in other people's files.
  • Thou shalt not use a computer to steal.
  • Thou shalt not use a computer to bear false witness.
  • Thou shalt not copy or use proprietary software for which you have not paid.
  • Thou shalt not use other people's computer resources without authorization.
  • Thou shalt not appropriate other people's intellectual output.

3. Discuss the role of computer ethics in the formation of a secure and reliable digital society.

Computer ethics play a crucial role in ensuring that digital interactions are fair, legal, and safe. By adhering to ethical guidelines, individuals and organizations contribute to a more secure, respectful, and trustworthy digital society, where privacy is respected, intellectual property is protected, and online interactions are responsible and ethical.

4. Explain the basic principles/components of information security.

The three main principles of information security are:
Confidentiality: Ensuring that information is only accessible to authorized individuals.
Integrity: Maintaining the accuracy and reliability of data.
Availability: Ensuring that information is accessible and usable when needed.

5. How do you know that your computer has been the victim of a malware attack?

- Slow performance or system crashes.
- Unexplained pop-ups or error messages.
- Unusual hard drive activity.
- Unauthorized changes to system settings.
- Strange files or programs appearing on your system.
- Antivirus software being disabled or compromised.

6. Define cyber law. What are the major fields of cyber law?

Cyber Law: A set of laws designed to regulate and protect individuals and organizations in the digital world.
Major fields of cyber law:

  • Privacy Law
  • Intellectual Property Law
  • Cybercrime Law
  • Electronic Commerce Law
  • Digital Signatures and Authentication

7. How can you protect yourself and your computer system from cybercrime?

- Use strong passwords and change them regularly.
- Install and update antivirus software.
- Avoid clicking on suspicious links or attachments in emails.
- Enable firewalls for additional security.
- Regularly back up important data.
- Be cautious while using public Wi-Fi.

8. What are the advantages and disadvantages of digital signature?

Advantages:
- Ensures data authenticity and integrity.
- Reduces the risk of fraud and tampering.
- Provides legally recognized proof of identity.
Disadvantages:
- Requires reliable digital infrastructure.
- May face resistance in regions with low digital literacy.
- Vulnerable to cyberattacks if not properly secured.

9. Explain various areas that are generally covered in cyber law.

- Protection of data and privacy.
- Intellectual property protection in digital media.
- Cybercrimes, such as hacking and identity theft.
- E-commerce laws and online transactions.
- Digital signatures and electronic contracts.

10. Explain cyber law in Nepal with major provisions included.

Cyber law in Nepal aims to regulate online activities, protect digital rights, and prevent cybercrimes. Major provisions include:

  • Protection of data and privacy rights.
  • Regulation of electronic transactions.
  • Establishment of penalties for cybercrimes such as hacking, identity theft, and cyberbullying.
  • Legal recognition of digital signatures and electronic contracts.

11. List out the major objectives of ICT Policy 2072.

- To improve the infrastructure for ICT development.
- To enhance digital literacy among the population.
- To ensure data privacy and security.
- To promote e-governance.
- To encourage innovation and research in the field of ICT.

1. Explain at least ten activities that are considered as cybercrime.

- Hacking into computer systems or networks.
- Identity theft and fraudulent activities.
- Phishing attacks to steal personal information.
- Malware attacks, such as viruses, worms, and ransomware.
- Cyberstalking and harassment.
- Child pornography distribution online.
- Cyberbullying.
- Piracy and illegal downloading of content.
- Denial of Service (DoS) attacks.
- Data breaches and unauthorized access to sensitive data.

2. What do you mean by malicious software? Explain any ten malicious software attacks.

Malicious Software (Malware): Software designed to harm, exploit, or otherwise compromise a computer system.
Types of Malware Attacks:

  • Virus: A program that attaches itself to files and spreads.
  • Worm: A self-replicating program that spreads over networks.
  • Trojan Horse: Disguises as a legitimate program to gain unauthorized access.
  • Ransomware: Encrypts the victim's data and demands payment for decryption.
  • Spyware: Collects sensitive information without the user's knowledge.
  • Adware: Unwanted advertisements that can compromise privacy.
  • Keylogger: Records keystrokes to steal login credentials.
  • Rootkit: A tool for gaining unauthorized access to a computer system.
  • Botnet: A network of infected computers used for malicious purposes.
  • Backdoor: A method of bypassing normal authentication to access a system.

3. Explain intellectual property along with its types in detail. What legal provisions we have in Nepal to preserve intellectual property rights?

Intellectual Property (IP): Legal rights that result from intellectual activity in industrial, scientific, literary, or artistic fields.
Types of Intellectual Property:

  • Copyright: Protection for original works of authorship such as books, music, and software.
  • Patent: Protection for inventions that are novel, useful, and non-obvious.
  • Trademark: Protection for symbols, logos, and brand names used in commerce.
  • Trade Secrets: Protection for confidential business information, such as formulas or processes.
Legal Provisions in Nepal: Nepal has provisions for the protection of intellectual property under the Copyright Act, the Patents Act, and the Trademark Act.

4. Explain digital signature along with its working mechanism.

A Digital Signature is a cryptographic technique used to authenticate the identity of the sender and ensure the integrity of the message.
Working Mechanism: - The sender creates a hash of the message and encrypts it with their private key.
- The recipient decrypts the signature with the sender's public key and verifies the hash.
- If the decrypted hash matches the message hash, the message is authentic and hasn't been tampered with.
