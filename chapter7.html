Chapter 7:Multimedia

Introduction to Multimedia

Multimedia consists of two words: ‘Multi’ and ‘Media’, where "multi" means many and "media" means a way/medium of expressing information. Hence, multimedia is a way of expressing information in different forms such as text, audio, video, graphics, and animation. Various forms of media are used for better understanding and effective communication. With multimedia, any information can be illustrated in greater depth.

Advantages of Multimedia

  • It helps to make communication much more effective.
  • It enhances the level of understanding in any particular topic.
  • Games, cartoons, and movies are made more effective and realistic by using multimedia.
  • It makes the teaching and learning process more interesting and intuitive.
  • Virtual reality and simulation have become more realistic.

Disadvantages of Multimedia

  • Multimedia components may be expensive.
  • Technical knowledge and IT literacy are required to operate multimedia.
  • Technology is always changing and never constant.
  • Multimedia devices run on electricity.

Components of Multimedia

  • Text: It is the collection of alphanumeric values or characters. It can be a word, a single line, or a paragraph. Text is the basic way of expressing information. Multimedia software allows customization of text in terms of size, color, type, and style. Common file extensions: .docx, .txt.
  • Graphics / Images: "A picture is worth a thousand words," meaning images can describe things more effectively than text. Graphics refer to images or pictures, where the smallest element is a pixel (Picture Element). The quality of an image is determined by its resolution. Common software: Adobe Photoshop, CorelDRAW. Common file extensions: .jpeg, .png.
  • Audio: One of the essential components of multimedia, providing the sensation of hearing. Audio can be human voice, instrument notes, natural sounds, or digitally synthesized sound. FM radio uses this component for broadcasting. Common software: Windows Media Player. Common file extensions: .mp3, .wma.
  • Video: Video consists of a sequence of rapidly displayed static images, measured in frames per second (FPS). A smooth video typically requires 15-25 FPS, while 30 or 60 FPS is the standard for high-quality playback. Common software: iMovie, Final Cut Pro, Filmora. Common file extensions: .mp4, .mpeg.
  • Animation: Animation is a sequence of computer-generated images displayed rapidly to create visual effects. It is widely used in movies and entertainment to depict ideas that are difficult to express in real life. There are two types of animation: 2D and 3D. Common software: Maya, AutoCAD, Macromedia Flash. Common file extensions: .flv, .gif.

Applications of Multimedia

  • Education
  • Communication
  • Entertainment
  • Medicine
  • Science and Technology
  • Advertisement
  • Websites/Webpages
  • Research and Engineering
